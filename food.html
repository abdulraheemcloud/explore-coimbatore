What is Kongu Cuisine?

Kongu cuisine hails from the Kongu Nadu region of Tamil Nadu, known for its use of freshwater fish, jungle herbs, and coconut-based gravies. Unlike coastal Tamil cooking, Kongu food features earthier spice profiles ‚Äî less chilli heat, more aromatic warmth from kalpasi, marathi mokku, and star anise.

The legendary institution of Coimbatore dining since 1951. Their special mutton biryani cooked in aged cast-iron vessels is the city's ultimate food pilgrimage.

A no-frills biryani paradise near Ukkadam market where the chicken biryani is cooked in sealed degchi pots over direct flame ‚Äî old school and absolutely magnificent.

Street Food

Shredded flaky parotta stir-fried with egg, masala, and your choice of meat on a giant iron griddle ‚Äî the rhythmic clanking sound is the soundtrack of Coimbatore nights.

Street Food

Annapoorna Sojhi Idli

The softest, fluffiest idli in South India served with coconut chutney and sojhi (a halwa-like sweet). This breakfast has made Annapoorna globally famous.

Beverage

Coimbatore's famous filter coffee ‚Äî freshly brewed decoction mixed with steamed milk and served in stainless steel tumblers. Poured from a great height for froth perfection.
